# Case Study: Phishing Attack

## Introduction

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers pretend to be
trusted organisations to trick people into revealing confidential
details such as passwords, OTPs, or banking information. These scams
often appear very genuine and create panic so that victims act quickly
without verifying authenticity.

## Example Incident

During 2020, several universities in India experienced a major phishing
campaign that targeted both students and faculty members. Victims
received emails that looked like they were sent from the official
university IT department. The message warned users that their login
access would expire within 24 hours and asked them to click a link to
update their credentials.

The provided link redirected users to a fake website designed to closely
resemble the real university login page. Many students entered their
usernames and passwords, unknowingly sharing their login information
with cybercriminals. Attackers later used these stolen credentials to
access university systems, collect personal data, and sometimes misuse
connected payment portals.

## Step-by-Step Process of the Attack

1.  The victim receives an email that appears to be sent from the
    university IT support team.
2.  The email creates urgency by stating that the account will expire
    soon.
3.  A link is provided that redirects the victim to a fake but
    realistic-looking login page.
4.  The victim enters their login ID and password.
5.  The attacker captures the login details immediately.
6.  The attacker uses the stolen credentials to log in to the real
    system.
7.  Sensitive information such as academic data, personal records, and
    financial details may be accessed.

## Common Targets

-   First-year students who are unfamiliar with official email systems
-   Faculty members who have access to confidential institutional
    information
-   Students who have connected payment methods to university portals
-   Individuals who respond quickly to urgent-looking emails

## Impact of the Attack

-   Unauthorized access to academic accounts
-   Theft of personal information
-   Financial losses if payment details are misused
-   Identity theft risks
-   Stress and mental pressure for victims
-   In some situations, academic records were altered

## Preventive Measures

-   Carefully check the sender's actual email address instead of
    trusting the display name
-   Avoid clicking links directly from emails; manually type the
    official website address
-   Institutions should implement security measures like SPF and DMARC
    authentication
-   Students should receive awareness training about phishing during
    orientation programs
